A100 Troubleshooting — Common Faults & Error Codes

The A100 is a professional machine. Most faults have straightforward causes. This guide covers the most common issues, error codes, and what to do about them — before calling a repair partner.

⚠ Always perform pressure relief before any inspection or repair. Turn off, unplug, set Prime/Spray valve to PRIME, hold gun against metal pail, pull trigger. Open drain valve to release any residual pressure.

Error codes

E01
Control board fault. Unplug, wait 45 minutes (thermal reset), retry. If persistent: control board part 16X295 or motor part 17C799 — contact repair partner.
E02
Motor failure. Same as E01. Check for hardened paint or frozen pump before assuming board failure. Spin fan by hand — must rotate freely.
E05
Overcurrent / overload. Tip too large, paint too thick, or pump seized. Check tip size (max TRA621 for A100), thin paint 5%, check pump rotates freely.

Common faults

No pressure / motor runs but no spray Often DIY
Check 1: Prime/Spray valve in correct position (SPRAY). Check 2: Tip not clogged — rotate to UNCLOG, trigger briefly into waste pail, rotate back. Check 3: Fluid filter blocked (part 243080) — remove and clean. Check 4: Inlet screen blocked (part 187651) — remove suction tube fitting and clean screen.
Pressure loss / patchy coverage Often DIY
Most likely: Tip too large for paint viscosity. Try TRA619 instead of TRA621 for thinner paints. Also check: Inlet/outlet valves worn — replace displacement pump (part 17C487 for A100). Generator users: minimum 3500W regulated — unregulated generators cause pressure fluctuation.
Motor trips / thermal cutout Often DIY
Most common cause: Unregulated generator. Use 3500W regulated minimum. Also: Extension cord too thin or too long — 15m min 1.5mm², 30m min 2.5mm². Thermal reset: Switch off, unplug, wait minimum 45 minutes before restarting.
Paint sags / runs on surface Technique
Pressure too low or tip too large. Increase pressure. Try smaller orifice tip. Gun too close: maintain 30cm from surface. Gun speed: move at even pace — if you slow down, release trigger before stopping.
Pump loses prime / suction loss Often DIY
Check: Suction tube for cracks or kinks. Garden hose washer worn (part 115099). Suction tube not reaching bottom of container. Inlet screen partially blocked.
Leaking collar / fluid around pump rod Repair partner
O-ring FX-90 locking ring collar (part 16H137) — replace this O-ring first. If leak continues, displacement pump replacement needed (part 17C487). Contact repair partner — torque spec is 95 ±7 Nm.

A100-specific notes

TSL: The throat seal cup must be filled before every use. If the cup is empty during operation, pump rod wear accelerates rapidly. TSL is a lubricant, not optional.

Voltage: A100 is 240V EU only. Never fit a 110V motor — it will fail immediately and may cause electrical damage. This is a safety-critical point.
